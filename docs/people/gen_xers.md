Generation X, also known as Gen Xers, refers to the demographic cohort born between approximately 1965 and 1980 [[Generation X]]. This generation is often characterized by their coming of age during a period of significant cultural, economic, and technological changes.

A substantial portion of Generation Xers are urban professionals. Urban professionals are individuals who live and work in cities, typically holding white-collar jobs such as lawyers, doctors, engineers, and journalists [[Urban Professional]]. This group is often associated with a certain level of financial stability and educational attainment.

The relationship between Generation X and urban professionalism is closely tied to the socioeconomic changes that occurred during their formative years. The 1980s saw a resurgence in city life, particularly in North America and Europe, as industrial cities transformed into vibrant centers of commerce, culture, and technology [[Urban Renewal]]. This shift provided opportunities for young professionals, many of whom were Gen Xers, to establish themselves in urban environments.
