**Apollo Wiki: Dinesh D'Souza**

Dinesh D'Souza is an American political commentator, author, and filmmaker, born on April 25, 1961, in Bombay, India. He moved to the United States as a teenager and graduated from Dartmouth College before earning his Ph.D. from Oxford University. D'Souza gained prominence in conservative political circles with the publication of his books, including "Illiberal Education" (1991), which criticized liberal academia, and "The Roots of Obama's Rage" (2010), a controversial analysis of U.S. President Barack Obama's political ideology.

In 2016, D'Souza released the documentary film "Hillary's America: The Secret History of the Democratic Party," which was criticized for its historical inaccuracies and biased portrayal of the Democratic Party. In 2018, he pleaded guilty to violating federal campaign finance laws and was sentenced to five years of probation, a $30,000 fine, and eight months in a community confinement center.

D'Souza's latest film, based on his book "Death of a Nation," was released in 2018. The film draws parallels between the current political climate and the Civil War era, drawing criticism for its historically debatable comparisons and provocative rhetoric. The film is currently showing in theaters.

D'Souza's work has been both celebrated and criticized, with supporters praising his conservative views and critics accusing him of spreading misinformation and using inflammatory language. His films have generated controversy and sparked debate, making D'Souza a controversial figure in American politics and media.
