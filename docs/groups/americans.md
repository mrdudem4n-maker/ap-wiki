**Kamala Harris**

Kamala Devi Harris (born October 20, 1964) is an American politician and attorney who served as the 32nd vice president of the United States from January 20, 2021, to January 20, 2025. She was the first woman, the first African American, and the first Asian American to hold this position. Prior to her vice presidency, Harris served as a U.S. Senator for California from 2017 to 2021.

Harris was born in Oakland, California, to parents who emigrated from India and Jamaica. She attended Howard University before graduating from the University of California, Hastings College of the Law. After working as a deputy district attorney in Alameda County, Harris spent several years in private practice, focusing on prosecuting child sexual abuse cases.

In 2010, Harris was elected District Attorney of San Francisco, becoming the first African American and the first woman to hold this position. In 2011, she moved to become California's Attorney General, a role she held until her election to the Senate in 2016.

As a Senator, Harris focused on civil rights, criminal justice reform, and health care policy. She gained national attention for her questioning of political figures during congressional hearings. In 2020, Harris ran as the Democratic nominee for President of the United States but dropped out before the primaries.

In August 2020, Harris was selected as Joe Biden's running mate for the presidential election. If elected, Harris would become the first woman to assume the office of vice president. The ticket won the election, and Harris was inaugurated on January 20, 2021. During her time in office, Harris focused on issues such as climate change, immigration reform, and addressing racial and social inequalities.

Harris's tenure as Vice President ended on January 20, 2025, with the inauguration of Joe Biden's successor. Since leaving office, she has remained active in political circles and has been mentioned as a potential candidate for the 2024 presidential election.

Harris's vice presidency was marked by her efforts to champion progressive policies and address long-standing social issues. Her tenure will likely be remembered as a significant milestone in American politics, representing a break from traditional norms and setting a precedent for future female and minority leadership.
