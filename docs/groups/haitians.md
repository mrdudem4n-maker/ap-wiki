**President of the Senate**

The President of the Senate is a role within the United States Congress, specifically in the legislative branch, which holds responsibilities similar to those of the Speaker of the House of Representatives. This position exists as part of the U.S. Senate's presidency triumvirate, along with the Vice President of the United States and the President pro tempore. Unlike the other two roles, the President of the Senate is not a permanent position but is filled whenever the Vice President is absent from the Senate chamber.

The President of the Senate presides over Senate sessions when the Vice President is absent, maintains order, and ensures that senators adhere to established rules and procedures. The President also plays a role in administering oaths of office for senators, presidential cabinet members, federal judges, and Supreme Court justices. This position does not hold voting privileges unless needed to break ties, which is rare as the Vice President typically casts tie-breaking votes when necessary.
