The concept was first discussed in online forums, with the intention of creating a more efficient version of Dogecoin while maintaining its playful and community-oriented spirit.

The proposed hard fork is known as "Apollo" due to its focus on improving the speed of transactions, much like Apollo, the ancient Greek god associated with progress and advancement. The name serves as a nod to the original Dogecoin's meme origins while emphasizing the improvements planned for the new blockchain.

The proposal suggests implementing a slower block time compared to the current 1-minute interval of Dogecoin. This change is intended to reduce network congestion and improve transaction processing speed, making Apollo potentially more suitable for everyday transactions and micro-payments. However, it's essential to note that the specific block time and other technical details have yet to be finalized.

Another proposed aspect of the Apollo hard fork is an undervote system, which could incentivize miners to support certain transactions or proposals by reducing their mining reward based on their voting behavior. This mechanism aims to promote decentralization and give more power to the community in determining the direction of the blockchain's development.

It's important to highlight that, as of now, the Apollo hard fork is still a proposal and not yet officially implemented on the Dogecoin network. The success of the project will depend on the level of community support and adoption, as well as the technical challenges that need to be addressed during the development process.

In summary, the Apollo hard fork is an initiative aimed at improving Dogecoin's transaction speed and reducing congestion by implementing a slower block time and potentially other modifications like the undervote system. The proposal is currently under discussion within the Dogecoin community, and its future will depend on the level of support and technical challenges that arise during development.
